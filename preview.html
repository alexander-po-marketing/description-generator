Semaglutide is a long-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist developed for once-weekly subcutaneous administration and once-daily oral dosing, targeting glycemic control and weight reduction in type 2 diabetes and obesity populations.

Semaglutide is indicated to improve glycemic control in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus as an adjunct to diet and exercise. It is not used in type 1 diabetes, diabetic ketoacidosis, or as initial therapy when lifestyle modification alone may suffice. The molecule also carries regulatory approvals for chronic weight management in adults with obesity or overweight with at least one weight-related comorbidity, and in defined pediatric populations.

Clinically, semaglutide has demonstrated robust reductions in HbA1c, fasting plasma glucose, body weight and selected cardiovascular risk markers across multiple phase 3 trial programs.

Semaglutide is a polypeptide-based GLP-1 analogue classified under A10BJ06 and belongs to the broader class of organic polypeptides used in diabetes management.

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist that enhances glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppresses glucagon, slows gastric emptying and modulates appetite, contributing to glycemic control, weight reduction and cardiometabolic benefits.

Mechanism of action

GLP-1 is a physiological hormone that promotes glycemic control via increased insulin secretion, slowed gastric emptying and reduced postprandial glucagon secretion. Semaglutide shares high sequence homology with native GLP-1 and selectively activates the GLP-1 receptor, stimulating pancreatic islet cells to increase insulin synthesis and reduce inappropriate glucagon release in a glucose-dependent fashion.

Pharmacodynamics

Treatment with semaglutide lowers HbA1c, fasting and postprandial plasma glucose, reduces body weight and modestly improves triglycerides and VLDL cholesterol. Safety-relevant pharmacodynamic effects include delayed gastric emptying, which may influence the absorption of concomitantly administered oral drugs.

High-molecular-weight peptide requiring careful control of temperature, agitation and gastric conditions depending on route of administration.

  • Semaglutide is formulated primarily as a subcutaneous aqueous solution and should be protected from elevated temperatures and excessive agitation to preserve structural integrity.
  • Oral tablets rely on absorption enhancers and must be administered under controlled gastric conditions (e.g., fasting state with limited water) to secure reproducible exposure.
  • Extreme hydrophilicity and low permeability shift formulation priorities towards maintaining peptide stability and minimizing degradation.

Semaglutide is an approved GLP-1 analogue with a maturing patent landscape, marketed across major regulated markets and moving from mid- to late-lifecycle.

Most listed U.S. patents on semaglutide have expired, with remaining protection extending into the late 2020s and at least one patent reaching into the early 2030s. This points to a product in transition from mid- to late-lifecycle, with generic competition constrained by staggered IP expiry.

A single originator currently supplies the API and finished products in the US, Canada and EU. Broader generic participation will depend on the timing and scope of remaining patent and exclusivity protections, as well as complex peptide manufacturing capabilities.

Key risks include potential pancreatitis, dehydration and theoretical thyroid C-cell tumors, combined with a long elimination half-life requiring extended monitoring in overdose scenarios.

  • Overdose exposures up to 4 mg mainly caused gastrointestinal events such as nausea.
  • No specific antidote is available; management is supportive.
  • Elimination half-life of about one week may require prolonged observation.

Product labels caution against use in patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma, and recommend immediate medical assessment for signs of pancreatitis, such as persistent severe abdominal pain. Delayed gastric emptying may alter the absorption profile of co-administered oral medicines.
